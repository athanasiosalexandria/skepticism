Skeptic Arguments

General Notes:

  • You don’t have to defend any physics, logic, or ethics as a skeptic
  • There are 2 types of skeptics, Pyrrhonian and Academic. We should decide on one in order to be consistent in our arguments.
  • Pyrrhonian skeptics say they don’t know anything, including whether knowledge is truly possible.
  • Academic skeptics say knowledge is impossible.
  • I think Pyrrhonian would be the stronger of the two because someone could simply ask the Academic the following: “How do you know that knowledge is impossible if knowledge is impossible?”
  • If you don’t have a prepared argument, you can literally just question how they know what they know–e.g. How they know their physics or ethics are true.. Eventually they won’t be able to give an answer beyond “Chrysippus said so” or “Epicurus said so”
  • Prof. Johnson said attacks on ethics are the best because people join Epicureanism or Stoicism mostly for the ethics, not the physics or logic.

Offensive Arguments:

Skeptics vs. Epicureans

  • Get an Epicurean to admit they believe in days of the week, beauty, color, or similar concepts. Argue for these concepts from a Platonic perspective. If they admit it, then you can tell them that Epicureanism is atomist/materialist and doesn’t believe in these universals so they contradicted themselves.
  • Ask how they know that the feeling of pleasure isn’t merely a byproduct of pursuing the τελος of life and not the τελος itself
  • Attack their view of friendship

Skeptics vs. Stoics

  • Attack their view of friendship

Defensive Arguments

  1. Q: “You keep making statements about things. Doesn’t that mean that you know things? That’s contradictory”
  • A: Just because I say something doesn’t mean I know it for a fact. I could be lying, or misremembering.

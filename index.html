Skeptic Arguments

General Notes:

  • You don’t have to defend any physics, logic, or ethics as a skeptic.
  • As a Skeptic you can use Epicurean arguments against Stoics and Stoic arguments against Epicureans.
  • There are 2 types of skeptics, Pyrrhonian and Academic. We should decide on one in order to be consistent in our arguments.
  • Pyrrhonian skeptics say they don’t know anything, including whether knowledge is truly possible.
  • Academic skeptics say knowledge is impossible.
  • I think the Pyrrhonian would be the stronger of the two because someone could simply ask the Academic the following: “How do you know that knowledge is impossible if knowledge is impossible?”
  • If you don’t have a prepared argument, you can literally just question how they know what they know–e.g. how they know their physics or ethics are true. Eventually they won’t be able to give an answer beyond “Chrysippus said so” or “Epicurus said so”
  • Prof. Johnson said attacks on ethics are the best because people join Epicureanism or Stoicism mostly for the ethics, not the physics or logic.

Offensive Arguments:

Skeptics vs. Epicureans

  • Attack their view of materialism/atomism – Get an Epicurean to admit they believe in days of the week, beauty, color, numbers, or similar concepts. Argue for these concepts from a Platonic perspective. If they admit it, then you can tell them that Epicureanism is atomist/materialist and doesn’t believe in these non-material universals so they contradicted themselves.
  • Attack their teleology – Ask how they know that the feeling of pleasure isn’t merely a byproduct of pursuing the τελος of life and not the τελος itself?
  • Attack their view of friendship – “are friends a tool to pleasure? Isn’t it counterintuitive to your pleasure to openly hold this position, and thus discourage people from wanting to be friends with you? Formally, you might be an Epicurean, but in practice, what is the difference between you and a sociopath?”
  • Attack Empiricism – How can you empirically sense that the world is atoms and void? How can you empirically sense that there is no afterlife? How does Empiricism allow you to sense Empiricism? Animals have all 5 senses and yet they aren’t cognizant that they are Empiricists, so how can it follow that Empiricism demonstrates Empiricism?

Skeptics vs. Stoics

  • Is humility a virtue to Stoics? Is it humble, then, to claim to know the esoteric inner workings of the universe, reason, and the goal of life without even providing justification, or is it hubris?
  • Argue against determinism – If our fate is determined from the beginning of the new universe, and we have lived the same exact lives every time the universe conflagrates, why should I even bother trying to become a sage? If it was written in destiny for me NOT to become a sage, there is nothing I can do to change that, not even as a Stoic. And if there is something I may do to change that, then that would mean free will, not determinism.
  • Attack their view of friendship – what even is the Stoic view on friendship? What would I pick in the dilemma between abandoning a friend who makes it harder to pursue virtue vs. maintaining the virtue of patience with that friend instead of cutting him off, since all virtue is valued the same?
  • Argue from ridiculousness – Why is all good equal and all evil equal? Should I feel like Mother Theresa after throwing a plastic bottle in the recycling bin? Should I feel like a murderer just because I stole $5 that I found on the sidewalk?
  • Great defense point: Chrysippus himself said it is APPROPRIATE for people who want to suspend judgment to do so (i.e. Skeptics) according to Plutarch’s Stoic Self-Contradictions (1036a). Why would a Stoic even argue against a Skeptic when Chrysippus is conceding that Skepticism is good for Skeptics?
  • Argue from lack of distinct dogma – who is a Stoic supposed to listen to: Chrysippus, Zeno, Epictetus, or Seneca? Many Stoics disagree on the fundamentals – e.g. Chrysippus believes the wise man’s soul continues after death but the others don’t, Ariston of Chios believes Stoics should do away with physics and logic but the others value all 3 etc.
  • Argue from Socrates – Socrates, whom Stoics practically worship, believed in OUR position with his famous quote “I know that I know nothing.” His line of questioning most closely resembled SKEPTICISM. He was fundamentally a SKEPTIC, not a Stoic.

Defensive Arguments

  1. Q: “You keep making statements about things. Doesn’t that mean that you know things? That’s contradictory”
  • A: Just because I say something doesn’t mean I know it for a fact. I could be lying, or misremembering, or maybe I’m misinformed etc.”
  1. Q: “Do you know what your name is? How could you not?”
  • A: Define “name.” If it is what people call me, does that mean that my name is “stupid” if someone calls me stupid?
  • A: [If speaking to an Epicurean] You guys don’t even believe in universals. No names are made of atoms. So you’re contradicting your position
  1. Q: “Don’t you believe the evidence of the 5 senses? They are right in front of you; how could you not be an Empiricist?
  • A: How does Empiricism demonstrate Empiricism? How can you gather that you have 5 senses by using your senses? You can’t argue from thought, because thought isn’t a sense.
  • A: The senses could always be grossly wrong. I could be hallucinating, or witnessing an optical illusion or magic trick,
  • A: [to an Epicurean] Epicureans believe that
  1. The Infinite Regress (last resort)
  • The only way a Skeptic can be criticized is if the Skeptic contradicts by admitting they know something or don’t know something, therefore knowledge is possible
  • If you say “I don’t know” or “I know” and get called out for it, refer to “Just because I say something doesn’t mean I know it for a fact. I could be lying, or misremembering, or maybe I’m misinformed etc.”
  • If you’re asked “how do you know that you can’t know anything for sure,” say “that’s the point of Skepticism, I can’t know anything for sure, so I can’t even even know that statement is sure.” Explain that it’s an infinite regress and that does the leg work to make any possible “how do you know” question invalid because you can’t know anything.

        etc… The burden of proof is not on Skeptics because we don’t propose anything; the burden of proof is on Epicureans and Stoics to prove their physics, logic, and ethics.

Closing Speech

  • In either case, both schools approach human behavior and ultimate purpose with a level of certainty unfitting for the support they use to back their claims
  • Are there not exceptions to every rule? In the Epicurean instance, they have failed to provide any evidence that pleasure or the absence of pain is continuous and intermediate states are non-existent. They have also failed to affirm that it is pleasure they seek and not some other internal factor. You cannot say with certainty what you feel is pleasure and cannot even define it yourself.
  • In the Stoic instance, they have failed to provide any evidence that adhering to virtue is sustainable in the cases of friendship or in its most basic state for the sake of character. You cannot say with certainty all virtues are equal or come up with supporting evidence that isn’t immediately met with a counter-example because you underestimate the complexity of virtue.
  • Skeptics approach human behavior and ultimate purpose with the level of uncertainty it requires
  • There is no absolute answer because we cannot provide inarguable evidence for it and accept that there are exceptions that invalidate the very suggestion there is an absolute answer. We also accept that the truth regarding human purpose may or may not be attainable to us because the justification for it might also be. There are numerous possibilities, and unlike these other two schools, we don’t deny this uncertainty confounds any universal statements.
  • We suspend judgement on all things because of this and can instead provide you with tranquility and the assurance that your beliefs will not be questioned.

Try to include this in the speech. Skeptics give you the most freedom from pain than the other two schools because we advocate that since you can’t know anything with certainty you suspend judgment on all things, and you don’t have to worry about your beliefs being questioned ever
